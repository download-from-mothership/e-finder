## Redaction Pattern Analysis

### Pattern 1: Systematic Third-Party Identity Protection
Across travel records (EFTA02847772, EFTA02847907), co-travelers and crew are consistently redacted while Epstein's information remains visible. This suggests FOIA processing determined Epstein (as the subject) has reduced privacy interest while third parties retain full (b)(6) protection. **This pattern could be challenged** — the identities of co-travelers on private aircraft may be of significant public interest.

### Pattern 2: Law Enforcement Technique Blanket
The (b)(7)(E) exemption dominates CBP system records (EFTA02848586, EFTA02847823, EFTA02848081). This exemption protects "techniques and procedures for law enforcement investigations or prosecutions" and is applied uniformly to system field labels, query parameters, and processing codes. Much of this appears to be **protecting the CBP computer system interface** rather than sensitive investigative techniques.

### Pattern 3: Victim Protection
EFTA02846673 represents a categorical decision to protect all victim/witness identities — the entire document is effectively a numbered list with no names. This is the one area where redaction seems clearly appropriate and unlikely to be challengeable.

### Pattern 4: Inconsistent Redaction Depth
Some documents (EFTA02846460, EFTA02846457) have no coded redactions at all, despite containing evidence details and government exhibits. This inconsistency suggests either: different FOIA processing offices handled different document batches, or these documents were already publicly released through litigation (the Maxwell trial exhibit marking on EFTA02846460 supports this).
